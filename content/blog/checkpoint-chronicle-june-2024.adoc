== Data Ecosystem

=== Catalogs
* Databricks and Snowflake each announced the intention to open-source their catalog implementations ( link:https://www.databricks.com/blog/open-sourcing-unity-catalog[Unity] and link:https://www.snowflake.com/blog/introducing-polaris-catalog/[Polaris], respectively).
* Polaris is not yet available open-source, but there's a nice write up from Sem Sinchenko of link:https://semyonsinchenko.github.io/ssinchenko/post/uniticatalog-first-look/[their initial experiences with Unity].
* Chris Riccomini has link:https://materializedview.io/p/begun-the-catalog-wars-have[a good explainer of the importance of catalogs] in data architectures.


=== Table Formats
* As well as announcing the open-sourcing of Unity, Databricks link:https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/databricks-agrees-acquire-tabular-company-founded-original-creators[announced the acquisition of Tabular]. This is the company founded by Ryan Blue, the original creator of Apache Iceberg. What this means for Iceberg is going to be fascinating to see. Will Databricks use the Iceberg expertise that they've now got to advance the format in parallel with their own Delta Lake, or perhaps just evolve intra-compatibility?
* If you're not familiar with Iceberg, there's link:https://seattledataguy.substack.com/p/apache-iceberg-what-is-it[a nice primer] from Seattle Data Guy, and you can see it in action in this blog that I wrote about link:https://www.decodable.co/blog/kafka-to-iceberg-with-decodable[writing Kafka data to Iceberg].
* There's an interesting analysis from Confluent‚Äîwho are backing Iceberg with their TableFlow product‚Äîlooking at whether link:https://medium.com/confluent/will-apache-iceberg-win-the-table-format-war-01f6ff0d556d?source=rss----09dcc6e07db7---4[Apache Iceberg will win over Delta Lake] (tl;dr: yes). That said, plenty of vendors are hedging their bets, with BigQuery recently announcing link:https://www.databricks.com/blog/bigquery-adds-first-party-support-delta-lake[first-party support for Delta Lake].
* Lastly, there were cries of ‚Äúdon't forget us!‚Äù from the Apache Hudi crowd, telling us that Hudi is link:https://www.onehouse.ai/blog/apache-hudi-the-open-data-lakehouse-platform-we-need[the Open Data Lakehouse Platform We Need].


=== And finally‚Ä¶
* link:https://redpanda.com/press/redpanda-acquires-benthos[Redpanda acquired Benthos] and swiftly rebranded it as RedPanda Connect with some licensing changes, prompting competitor WarpStream to link:https://www.warpstream.com/blog/announcing-bento-the-open-source-fork-of-the-project-formerly-known-as-benthos[fork it].
* In all of this, link:https://data.techcommunitycontent.com/shades-of-open-source-understanding-the-many-meanings-of-open-79b944363c07[Alex Merced's article about open source] is particularly timely. He explains well what open source means, how it's governed, and its relationship with commercial entities‚Äîrelevant to the catalog announcements, table formats, and Benthos acquisition.

== Event Streaming
* Walmart have been using Kafka for many years, and share some really useful experiences and suggestions in this article about link:https://medium.com/walmartglobaltech/reliably-processing-trillions-of-kafka-messages-per-day-23494f553ef9[Reliably Processing Trillions of Kafka Messages Per Day].
* An interesting look at how booking.com use link:https://medium.com/booking-com-development/events-as-the-4th-pillar-of-booking-observability-platform-f41480a68f5a?source=rss----1c36c35f9c76---4[events as part of their observability strategy].
* Getting metrics out of Kafka clients has previously been limited to what you could do with JMX. KIP-714 proposes improvements in this area, adding support for OpenTelemetry. My friend and former colleague Ricardo Ferreira has done a nice writeup of it in link:https://community.aws/content/2i3i0osdRihttgQ8AZii6F0Bv4F/kip-714-keep-your-kafka-clusters-close-and-your-kafka-clients-closer[this article].


== Data Platforms and Architecture
* As an active user of Reddit, and a mod over on the link:https://old.reddit.com/r/apachekafka/[r/apachekafka subreddit], I'm always particularly interested to learn more about the platform itself. This blog from Stephan Weinwurm and colleagues in the engineering team at Reddit explains link:https://www.reddit.com/r/RedditEng/comments/1dnklb6/enriching_data_for_reddit_safetys_rules_engine_in/[how they implemented and rolled out a new Python-based microservice based around Kafka] for scoring new content as its posted to check for any breach of Reddit's content rules.
* I wince every time I see someone give the hackneyed and generic ‚Äúfraud detection‚Äù as a reason for needing real-time data‚Äîso these examples from link:https://eng.lyft.com/eta-estimated-time-of-arrival-reliability-at-lyft-d4ca2720bda8?source=rss----25cd379abb8---4[Lyft] and link:https://www.uber.com/en-GB/blog/personalized-marketing-at-scale/[Uber] of how they use data are a really refreshing read.
* I was fascinated by link:https://engineering.atspotify.com/2024/04/data-platform-explained/[this teaser] from Spotify about more details of their data platform, and delighted when they published link:https://engineering.atspotify.com/2024/05/data-platform-explained-part-ii/[this followup] going into details of some of the design as well as technologies used, including Apache Flink and BigQuery.
* Uber are not mucking about when it comes to data volumes in their batch systems‚Äîover an exabyte is held across their estate of Hadoop (HDFS) servers. link:https://www.uber.com/en-GB/blog/modernizing-ubers-data-infrastructure-with-gcp/[This blog] details their migration from purely on-premises infrastructure to a hybrid deployment with GCP. Whilst adopting Google's object store (GCS) they are for now continuing to run their own software, with a move to native PaaS on GCP planned in for the future.
